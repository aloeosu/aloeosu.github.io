Experience

I started off this course learning GitHub and Markdown syntax. Then, we moved on to Khan Academy, learning about HTML and CSS. After that, we started to do Javascript. I've had almost no trouble completing my work and assignments on time during that period of time. Then we started Advanced JS. I flew through the first few lessons. I noticed that there were no more videos and only text. I was okay with that for a while. However, I started to get very confused after the first chunk of Advanced JS. The text was sometimes hard to understand and was way worse than a video of someone explaining it to you. I started to get behind on my work, sometimes spending an entire class just trying to figure 1 or 2 lessons. What made it worse was that I would sometimes end up forgetting what to code, so I would have had to go back and relearn it briefly. This made me fall really behind on my projects in Natural Simulations, which is the reason why I never got the chance to do Intro to SQL, jQuery, and Games and Visualizations. The summative was the next big project. I was in a group with GMAC and me and him decided to explore and code the mandelbrot set. I decided to look more into the math aspect of it and GMAC looked into the Computer Science part of it. In the end, We successfully coded a working mandelbrot set (some troubles in the live session) and both made our own spinoffs. I made certain values change depending on your mouseX and mouseY and that would result in a moving, pattern changing mandelbrot set. I got a huge feeling of accomplishment after leaning to successfully code the mandelbrot set and I believe it was the highlight of the course. After I finished the summative, I went back to Khan Academy to try and finish up the strands I haven't finished. However, I didn't really look at JS throughout the Christmas Break and as a result, I forgot alot of stuff, so I had to try to relearn. In the end, I think I did okay in the course, but time management was a big issue for me, which made me leave alot of projects and strands unfinished. Despite this, I did enjor the course and it was super fun coding things, and I did end up coding some pretty cool projects.
